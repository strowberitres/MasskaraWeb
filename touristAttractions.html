Bacolod City
Park
and Lagoon.

Bacolod Park and Lagoon, a city icon, features a peaceful lagoon with fish and turtles, framed by lush greenery and carabao sculptures. It's a perfect spot for picnics, walks, and cultural events, with local vendors and vibrant surroundings.

The Bacolod City New Government Center is the city's modern hub for public service. Its contemporary design and layout serve as the central venue for municipal administration, public hearings, and city events. The landscaped surroundings offer a serene backdrop, and nightly illumination adds to its visual charm.

The Bacolod City
Public Plaza.

The Bacolod Public Plaza is a major landmark and gathering spot in the heart of the city. This picturesque park offers a serene retreat from the hustle and bustle of city life. Visitors can take a leisurely stroll, relax on the benches, or simply soak in the charming ambiance. The plaza is also home to the iconic “Panaad Park and Stadium,” a popular venue for various events and celebrations

The San Sebastian Cathedral, among Bacolod City's oldest and most exquisite churches, stands as a symbol of the city's religious and cultural heritage. Constructed in the late 19th century, this architectural marvel boasts Romanesque design, showcasing stunning stained glass windows, intricate woodwork, and a bell tower with antique bells.

The Upper East.

The Upper East Bacolod is a cosmopolitan township development in Bacolod City. It is a master-planned community that integrates residential, commercial, educational, and leisure components. This modern urban district is designed with a focus on walkability and green spaces, offering a vibrant lifestyle destination for residents and visitors alike

The Negros Museum, once the 1925 Provincial Agriculture Building, now houses a private provincial museum displaying historical and contemporary local artworks. It preserves the cultural heritage of the Negros Islands, aims to educate the younger generation, and includes a café for visitors.
